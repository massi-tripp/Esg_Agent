2.2.2.6.1.2 Verification of compliance to DNSH criteria
Climate change adaptation – all activities
Following the TCFD approach, an assessment of climate risks
and vulnerability, based on three scenarios:
●New Green Deal +1.5°C scenario;
●Eco-Techno Driven + 3°C scenario;
●Retreat and fragmentation +4°C scenario,
was carried out to identify sites likely to be affected (See
chapter 2.2.2.1.2)
The identified physical climate risks have been assessed on
the basis of the lifetime of the asset concerned and are
essentially of three types (extreme heat, water stress and
flooding) covered by appropriate action plans. 
Climate change mitigation – activity 5.4
Direct
emissions
related
to
heat
production
on
remanufacturing sites are under 270 g CO2eq per kWh.
Transportation-related emissions are covered by Renault
Group’s decarbonization initiative. 
Sustainable use and protection of water and marine
resources – all activities
In addition to complying with applicable regulations and the
ban on using any non-renewable water source, the factories
are committed to reducing the quantities of water needed
to produce a vehicle (See chapter 2.2.2.2.4) and to reducing
toxic metal emissions that can impact water resources (See
chapter 2.2.2.3.4).
Transition to a circular economy –
all activities except 2.6 and 5.4
Renault Group’s circular economy policy (See chapter
2.2.2.5) covers DNSH criteria related to (a) reuse and use of
secondary raw materials and re-used components in
products manufactured, (b) design for high durability,
recyclability, easy disassembly and adaptability of products
manufactured, (c) waste management that prioritizes
recycling over disposal in the manufacturing process, and
(d) substances of concern which are identified and
monitored throughout the life cycle (se chapter 2.2.2.2.2) 
Low-carbon vehicles involved in activity 6.5 are reusable or
recyclable to a minimum of 85% by weight, reusable or
recoverable to a minimum of 95% by weight, the Group
complying with the European Directive 2000/53/EC on end-
of-life vehicles.

Renault Group contributes to and uses the International
Material Data System (IMDS), through which the Group
manages hazardous substances throughout the value
chain, from suppliers to customers. Additionally, other
functions within the Group use IMDS to comply with all
applicable regulations (REACH, CLP, POPs, etc.).
Renault Group’s substance policy meets criteria (a) to (e) of
Annex C of Delegated Regulation 2021/2139 (See Chapter
2.2.2.2.2), including the regulations governing the use of

components, ozone-depleting gases, whose use is already

applicable to the Group and its suppliers, and substances

For any substance of very high concern (SVHC) representing

Pollution prevention and control – other activities 
The low-emission vehicles that Renault Group sells,
finances, leases or operates are all equipped with tyres in
external road noise and resistance classes that comply with
the European requirements set out in EC Regulation 661/
2009. As the requirements of the Taxonomy go beyond
regulatory compliance on this criterion, an additional
analysis was conducted and demonstrated that most tyre
references originally fitted to low-emission vehicles meet
this criterion. However, despite the Group’s best efforts, it
was not possible to verify this for all the financed vehicles, as
information on their actual tyre fitment is not available. To
date, this criterion is considered inoperable. This position will
be reassessed in the future depending on the availability of
the necessary data. 
With a certified noise level well below 68 dBA, Renault
electric vehicles have met, since 2021, the external noise
limits applicable starting in 2024, thus contributing to the
reduction of ambient noise and the quality of life in urban
areas. All low-emission vehicles placed on the market by
Renault Group in Europe in 2024 therefore comply with
European regulation 540/2014/EC applicable to vehicles
certified since July 2016, which require a maximum of 72 dBA. 
Renault Group’s dismantling facilities are equipped to
manage and store safely and in an environmentally sound
manner hazardous substances, mixtures and components
removed during the depollution operations, and comply with
European regulation.
