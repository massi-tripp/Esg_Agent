The European regulatory landscape is rapidly increasing the volume and importance of corporate non-financial disclosures, including sustainability reports that describe environmental, social, and governance (ESG) practices and, in many cases, EU Taxonomy-related information. However, these disclosures are predominantly published as long, heterogeneous, and unstructured documents, which makes systematic analysis and large-scale benchmarking costly when performed manually.
\\
This thesis proposes an end-to-end Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) solution for the automated analysis of non-financial documentation with respect to the European regulatory framework, with a focus on extracting EU Taxonomy economic activities explicitly reported by companies. More specifically, the scope of the work is limited to the identification of disclosed economic activities and, when available, their associated Taxonomy references or codes. The thesis does not attempt to perform a full EU Taxonomy compliance assessment, nor to verify alignment conditions such as technical screening criteria, Do No Significant Harm (DNSH), minimum safeguards, or the correctness of reported KPI values.
\\
The proposed system is organized into three phases. First, an ESG report discovery agent identifies official 2024 annual reports starting from corporate domains. Second, a manually curated benchmark is constructed to enable rigorous evaluation of extraction quality. Third, multiple RAG variants are implemented and compared, exploring alternative retrieval and generation strategies to maximize evidence-grounded extraction of activities and taxonomy references.
\\
The resulting framework provides a reproducible pipeline to build structured datasets from real-world sustainability reports and offers empirical insights into failure modes and design choices for compliance-oriented RAG systems.
