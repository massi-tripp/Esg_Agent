Pollution prevention and control – other activities 
The low-emission vehicles that Renault Group sells,
finances, leases or operates are all equipped with tyres in
external road noise and resistance classes that comply with
the European requirements set out in EC Regulation 661/
2009. As the requirements of the Taxonomy go beyond
regulatory compliance on this criterion, an additional
analysis was conducted and demonstrated that most tyre
references originally fitted to low-emission vehicles meet
this criterion. However, despite the Group’s best efforts, it
was not possible to verify this for all the financed vehicles, as
information on their actual tyre fitment is not available. To
date, this criterion is considered inoperable. This position will
be reassessed in the future depending on the availability of
the necessary data. 
With a certified noise level well below 68 dBA, Renault
electric vehicles have met, since 2021, the external noise
limits applicable starting in 2024, thus contributing to the
reduction of ambient noise and the quality of life in urban
areas. All low-emission vehicles placed on the market by
Renault Group in Europe in 2024 therefore comply with
European regulation 540/2014/EC applicable to vehicles
certified since July 2016, which require a maximum of 72 dBA. 
Renault Group’s dismantling facilities are equipped to
manage and store safely and in an environmentally sound
manner hazardous substances, mixtures and components
removed during the depollution operations, and comply with
European regulation.

2.2.3.1.1 SBM-3 – Material Impacts,
Risks and Opportunities and
their interaction with strategy
and business model – transversal
Renault Group recognizes the diverse nature of its
workforce which includes employees and non-employees,
described below as workers.
The Group defines employees as all workers having an
employment contract (permanent and fixed-term contracts)
at the end of the reporting period within a consolidated entity
of the Group. Non-employees are temporary workers and
workers with no employment contract such as interns in
France (internship contract) or VIE (volunteer for international
experience). [DP S1.SBM-3_02]
2.2.3.1.2 S1-3 – Processes to prevent, mitigate
and remediate negative impacts
and channels for own workforce
to raise concerns – transversal
Renault Group has deployed a whistleblowing system,
WhistlB, which is a confidential and secure platform for
collecting reports, open to all employees (including
individuals seeking employment and former employees),
who can report facts contrary to the law, the Code of Ethics,
or the Anti-corruption Code of Conduct.
The WhistleB system is complemented by the regular
internal channels for reporting whistleblowing, which
include
management,
Human
Resources,
employee
representatives, the Ethics & Compliance Department and
the network of Ethics & Compliance officers.
For more information on WhistleB and the management of
whistleblowers, see chapter 2.2.4.1.2.2. [DP S1-3_01; DP S1-
3_02; DP S1-3_05 to 09]
2.2.3.1.3 Working conditions
and environment
2.2.3.1.3.1 SBM-3 – Material Impacts, Risks
and Opportunities and their interaction
with strategy and business model
A. Working conditions and human rights 
Ethics and sustainable development are central to Renault
Group’s strategic vision. The organization, both as an employer
and as an automotive manufacturer, is committed to
achieving excellence in the respect and promotion of human
rights. Renault Group ensures that its Purpose and values,
which are deeply ingrained among its workforce, are reflected
in its business practices. This alignment fosters a constructive
environment in the regions where it operates and strengthens
relationships with its partners. Furthermore, Renault Group
places a high priority on the capacity of its operational entities
to swiftly address and rectify any potential infringements on
human rights or fundamental freedoms that may arise from its
commercial activities. 
Renault Group acknowledges the material risks on its activity
arising from the potential impacts of human and labor rights
infringements on its own workforce. One key risk involves
potential remediation costs, penalties, and reputational
damage from non-compliance with working conditions
regulations and human rights, which can negatively affect
workers’ psychological and physical well-being. Additionally,
poor working conditions can lead to a loss of workforce
productivity, as they can have adverse psychological and
physical impacts on employees. [DP S1.SBM-3_05]
Renault Group is committed to enhancing the working
conditions that contribute to the psychological and physical
well-being of all its workers. [DP S1.SBM-3_01; DP S1.SBM-3_12]
Renault Group has developed an understanding of the
widespread impacts that may be faced by its workforce
through a comprehensive approach to human rights and
working conditions. However, Renault Group considers that,
except for specificities related to the type of task performed,
all individuals within its workforce are considered at equal risk
of harm. [DP S1.SBM-3_03; DP S1.SBM-3_11]
Renault Group has not identified any activity or geography
that presents a particularly high risk in terms of forced labor
or child labor. [DP S1.SBM-3_07 to 10]
B. Health and safety
Renault Group has a responsibility to protect its employees
by maintaining a secure workplace that prevents accidents
and work-related illnesses. Given the nature of its
operations, the types of tasks performed, and the materials
handled by its workforce, Renault Group faces numerous

A. Working conditions and human rights 
Renault Group’s human rights policy is aligned with key
documents such as the 1948 Universal Declaration of Human
Rights, the UN Global Compact principles, and the OECD

for
Multinational

The

Group working environment”, and to the International Labour
Organization (ILO) Conventions, ensuring the elimination of
forced labor and setting the minimum working age at 15, in
compliance with local laws.
